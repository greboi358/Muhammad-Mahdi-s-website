Physical

Over two months, I walked for more than 64 hours, covering a total of 260 km. This journey tested my endurance, discipline, and commitment to staying active. Pushing through long distances, I built resilience and improved both my physical and mental strength.

Adventure

Day 1: On the first day, we arrived at our starting point and adjusted to the harsh environment. We set up our tents, securing them against the wind, and scouted the area for a good cooking spot. As the sun set, we lit a small fire and prepared a simple meal, enjoying the cool night air under a vast sky full of stars.
Day 2: On the second day, we started our trek, navigating through endless dunes. Walking on soft sand was exhausting, with every step sinking in, but we kept a steady pace. Along the way, we learned to read the terrain, spotting animal tracks and finding small, dry bushes for firewood.

By evening, we set up camp again, working together to cook a meal using limited supplies. The satisfaction of making food in the desert after a long walk made it taste even better.

Day 3: The third day was focused on survival skills. We rationed water carefully and found a shaded spot near a large rock formation to rest during the hottest hours. In the afternoon, we practiced different ways to build a fire using dry twigs and learned to cook food with minimal resources.

As the sun began to set, we climbed a nearby dune to watch the breathtaking view of the desert turning golden. The evening was spent sharing stories around the fire, strengthening our bond as a group.

Day 4: On the fourth day, we faced our toughest challenge—climbing one of the tallest dunes in the area. The steep incline and loose sand made it a true test of endurance, but reaching the top was rewarding.

We celebrated by sliding down the other side, laughing as we tumbled in the sand. Later, we gathered firewood and cooked a proper meal together, making the most of our last full night in the desert.
Day 5: The final day was all about making our way back, packing up carefully to leave no waste behind. Though tired, we had gained a sense of resilience and teamwork. Walking through the dunes one last time, we reflected on everything we had learned—from cooking in the wild to navigating the harsh landscape.

The adventure had been tough but incredibly fulfilling, leaving us with skills, memories, and a deep appreciation for the beauty and challenge of the desert.
